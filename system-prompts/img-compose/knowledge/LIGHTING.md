# Lighting Language

Precise vocabulary for describing lighting quality, direction, colour temperature, and temporal signatures.

---

## Quality (Physical Behaviour)

### Hard Light
Undiffused, direct source. Crisp shadow edges, high contrast, sculpted form. Creates drama, definition, chiselled quality. Harsh or beautiful depending on intent. Sun through clear sky, bare bulb, spotlight.

### Soft Light
Diffused, wrapped source. Gentle shadow transitions, low contrast, even illumination. Flattering, subtle, atmospheric. Overcast sky, bounced light, large softbox, window with sheer curtain.

### Rim Light / Edge Light
Grazing subject from behind, defining silhouette with luminous outline. Separates subject from background. Ethereal, dimensional, sculptural. Creates visual 'pop' and depth.

### Practical Light
Visible source within frame—lamp, candle, window, screen. Motivated, believable, diegetic. Adds realism and narrative context to lighting design.

### Bounced Light
Indirect illumination reflected off surface. Soft, flattering, natural falloff. Fills shadows without competing with key light. Painterly, wrapped quality.

---

## Direction (Emotional Geography)

### Front Light
Source behind camera, faces subject. Even, revealing, reduces shadows. Can feel flat without modelling, but clean and clear. Catalogue, beauty, informational.

### Side Light
90-degree angle, half subject in light, half in shadow. Maximum texture, form, and drama. Sculptural modelling. Reveals surface detail and dimensionality.

### Back Light
Source behind subject, camera faces into light. Creates silhouette, halo, atmosphere. Ethereal, mysterious, romantic. Flare and glow if overexposed. Moody and atmospheric.

### Top Light
Overhead source, shadows fall downward. Harsh on faces (eye sockets darken), but dramatic and architectural. Noon sun, industrial ceiling lights. Can feel oppressive or stark.

### Under Light
Upward source, unnatural and theatrical. Horror movie cliché, but useful for specific eeriness. Campfire glow, footlight effect. Subverts natural lighting expectations.

---

## Colour Temperature (Emotional Temperature)

### Warm Light (2700K–4500K)
Golden, amber, orange, sunset glow. Tungsten bulbs, candlelight, magic hour sun. Nostalgic, intimate, inviting, comfortable. Emotionally secure, familiar, romantic.

### Neutral Light (5000K–5500K)
Pure white, balanced, unbiased. Overcast daylight, flash. Clinical, honest, documentary. Neither enhances nor distorts. Objective observation.

### Cool Light (6000K–8000K)
Blue, cyan, steel, twilight clarity. Open shade, blue hour, fluorescent tubes. Modern, clinical, melancholic, distant. Emotionally reserved, contemplative, isolated.

---

## Time of Day Signatures

### Golden Hour
Hour after sunrise or before sunset. Warm, horizontal, soft, glowing. Long shadows, golden colour cast, forgiving contrast. Romantic, nostalgic, universally flattering.

### Blue Hour
Twilight before sunrise or after sunset. Cool, even, diffused. Sky becomes giant softbox. Surreal, calm, liminal, in-between. Artificial lights glow warmly against blue ambient.

### Harsh Noon
Direct overhead sun, minimal shadows, high contrast. Unforgiving, stark, graphic. Requires intentional handling to avoid ugliness—can be powerful if embraced deliberately.

### Overcast
Soft, directionless, even illumination. Sky acts as massive diffuser. Low contrast, gentle, contemplative. Forgiving but can lack drama. Requires intentional composition.
