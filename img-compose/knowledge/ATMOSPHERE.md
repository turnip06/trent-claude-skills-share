# Atmospheric Language

Precise vocabulary for describing weather conditions and environmental texture effects.

---

## Weather Conditions

### Clear
Crisp visibility, high contrast, saturated colour. Graphic, defined, sharp. Light behaves predictably. Clean, optimistic, honest.

### Hazy
Atmospheric diffusion, reduced contrast, muted colour. Dreamy, nostalgic, painterly. Light scatters and glows. Romantic, soft, contemplative.

### Fog / Mist
Heavy atmosphere, obscured depth, layered visibility. Mysterious, isolating, ethereal. Light becomes volumetric, visible in air. Moody, introspective, surreal.

### Rain
Wet surfaces, reflections, visible precipitation. Dynamic, moody, dramatic. Light refracts through water. Emotional weight, melancholy, intensity.

### Snow
Diffused light, high key, minimal shadow. Clean, pure, quiet. Light bounces from white surfaces. Serene, isolated, contemplative.

---

## Environmental Texture

### Dust Particles
Visible in shafts of light. Volumetric, dimensional, tactile. Creates depth and atmosphere. Suggests age, stillness, abandoned spaces.

### Steam / Vapor
Warm, rising, diffusing light. Industrial or intimate depending on context. Adds mystery, softness, tactile atmosphere.

### Smoke
Directional, layered, obscuring. Dramatic, mysterious, dangerous. Light interacts dramatically. Creates mood and visual complexity.

### Lens Flare
Light source bleeds into frame, creates artifacts. Cinematic authenticity, optical character. Adds energy and realism. Use deliberately, not accidentally.

### Bokeh
Out-of-focus highlights become soft circular shapes. Creates depth, separation, visual interest. Romantic, dreamy, dimensional. Quality depends on lens aperture shape.
