# Colour & Material Language

Precise vocabulary for describing colour relationships, tonal character, surface qualities, and temporal rendering.

---

## Colour Palettes

### Monochromatic
Single hue with tonal variations. Unified, sophisticated, focused. Mood-driven rather than realistic. Requires strong composition to succeed.

### Complementary
Opposite colours on wheel (blue/orange, red/green). High contrast, vibrant, energetic. Naturally eye-catching. Most common in cinematic work.

### Analogous
Adjacent colours (blues through greens, oranges through reds). Harmonious, unified, gentle. Creates mood through colour agreement rather than contrast.

### Triadic
Three equally spaced colours. Complex, balanced, sophisticated. Harder to execute but visually rich when successful.

### Desaturated / Muted
Low colour intensity, near-neutral. Realistic, contemplative, documentary. Avoids visual fatigue, feels honest and grounded.

### Saturated / Vibrant
High colour intensity, punchy. Energetic, stylised, confident. Commands attention but can overwhelm if not balanced.

---

## Tonal Character

### High Key
Bright overall, minimal shadows, optimistic. Fashion, beauty, aspirational. Clean, positive, airy.

### Low Key
Dark overall, emphasised shadows, dramatic. Noir, thriller, intimate. Moody, mysterious, focused.

### Balanced Mid-Tone
Even distribution of light and shadow. Realistic, neutral, documentary. Neither optimistic nor pessimistic—observational.

---

## Material & Texture Language

### Surface Qualities

#### Matte
Non-reflective, absorbed light, subtle. Fabric, painted surfaces, skin. Soft, approachable, understated.

#### Glossy
Specular reflections, defined highlights. Polished surfaces, wet materials, glass. Sharp, modern, precise.

#### Metallic
Coloured reflections, conductive sheen. Chrome, gold, copper. Luxurious, technical, eye-catching.

#### Translucent
Light passes through but diffuses. Fabric, paper, frosted glass. Soft, mysterious, layered.

#### Transparent
Clear light transmission, visible through. Glass, water, crystal. Clean, pure, dimensional.

#### Rough / Textured
Irregular surface catching light variably. Stone, bark, fabric weave. Tactile, authentic, grounded.

---

## Motion & Time Language

### Static / Still
No movement, frozen moment. Contemplative, observational, composed. All detail sharp and readable.

### Implied Motion
Subject mid-action, captured sharply. Energy frozen but evident. Dynamic framing suggests movement beyond frame.

### Motion Blur
Subject blurred by movement during exposure. Dynamic, energetic, speed conveyed. Background sharp, subject streaked (or inverse).

### Shallow Depth of Field
Narrow focus plane, rest blurred. Selective focus, emotional emphasis. Dreamy, cinematic, directed attention.

### Deep Depth of Field
Everything in focus, front to back. Documentary clarity, environmental context. Informational, comprehensive, observational.
